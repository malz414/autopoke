Core Gameplay Mechanics

Stats Overlay Explanation

Each Pokémon has unique stats including HP, Defenses, Attack Speed, etc. Here is an example with a Rowlet:

  • Attack: 65
  • Defense: 95
  • Special Attack: 20
  • Special Defense: 110
  • Attack Speed: 0.90
  • Critical Hit Rate: 25%
  • Critical Hit Damage Bonus: 140%

Level Progression Rates

As you advance in levels, you gain more Pokémon on your board and unlock Unite moves, which also affects the appearance rate of certain cost Pokémon.

Level Rewards

Level up your trainer to increase the number of Pokémon on your board and gain Unite discs. Each round grants 4 experience points automatically, or invest 4 gold to gain 4 experience. The item Lucky Egg grants 2 bonus experience upon losing.

Currency Management

Currency is crucial for victory. Here's how to manage it effectively:

  • You gain a minimum of 9 currency in early rounds, reducing to 7 in later rounds.
  • Maintain a Winning or Losing streak (See below)
  • Equip an Amulet Coin to gain extra currency for match wins
  • Interest: Gain extra currency based on your current amount.
    • 10-19: 1 extra currency
    • 20-29: 2 additional currencies
    • 30+: 3 additional currencies

Streak Bonuses

Both winning and losing streaks grant additional currency:

  • 2 Streak: 1 extra currency
  • 3/4 Streak: 2 additional currencies
  • 5+ Streak: 3 additional currencies

Pokémon Star Levels

Each Pokémon has three versions: 1-Star, 2-Star, and 3-Star. You start with the 1-Star version from the shop. Collecting three 1-Star versions upgrades it to 2-Star, and three 2-Star versions upgrade it to 3-Star.

A 2-Star Pokémon unlocks additional abilities and may evolve. Its stats increase slightly, and it gains a second move swappable during preparation, though only one move can be swapped per round. With a Unite disc from Trainer level 5 or 8, the Pokémon can use both its chosen move and Unite move in battle.

A 3-Star Pokémon sees a significant stat boost and may evolve to its final form.
